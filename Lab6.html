e) Discuss the answer to part d in some depth. Is it symmetric? Is it biased? Does this make sense?

The change of signal strength is the same when moving 1 sigma from the center of the distriubtion to its left or right. So the true signal strength distribution should be approximately symmetric. The true signal distribution is biased because the observed data is 300 while the true signal is centered at 200.

The result makes sense:

Signal + background = data. Also, the background is approximately symmetric and is centered at 100. So, when data = 300,

P(signal=x) = P(background=300-x) = P(background=100-(300-x-100)) = P(background=x-100),

which is symmetric and centered at 200.
